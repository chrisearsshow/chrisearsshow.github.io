#40. Mating In Captivity Part 1.

Chris: Alright, I’ll let you introduce the book today.

Voice Of Judgement: “Mating in Captivity!” by Esther Perel!

C: And what’s it about?

VOJ: Why married people don’t have sex!

C: Umm… or how to keep sex interesting in a committed relationship.

VOJ: Do we need a trigger warning?

C: I doubt kids are listening to this but okay. We talk about sex.

VOJ: [Singing]…Baby, how about you and me, all the good times and the bad times, you and me…

C: So the book has a lot of insights so we’ll break it up in to a few episodes.

VOJ: Yay! Less reading!

C: Yeah, it’s been a bit brutal reading a book a week. Okay, starting with chapter 1. What did we learn here?

VOJ: Modern life means we’re more free but also more lonely. When we date, we want that to fix all the loneliness.

C: Yeah, which leads to wanting predictable relationships. But eroticism thrives on the unpredictable.

VOJ: So how do we fix it?

C: Chapter 2 suggests examining how intimacy and sex are related. More of one can mean less of the other.

VOJ: Aren’t the two supposed to go together?

C: Not in the author’s experience as a couple’s therapist. Sexual excitement requires the capacity not to worry and the pursuit of pleasure demands a degree of selfishness. Some people can’t allow themselves this selfishness because they’re too absorbed with the well-being of their partner.

VOJ: Huh, you care too much about them so you don’t want to have sex with them.

C: Yup. Aggression, objectification, and power all exist in the shadow of desire, components of passion that don’t necessarily nurture intimacy.

VOJ: So how do we fix that?

C: In chapter 3, the author suggests moving away from verbal communication. Relying too heavily on it limits how fully we can convey ourselves.

VOJ: [Singing] Use your body! Use my body!

C: Right. With one couple, the author has them do physical interactions like leading each other around the room, pushing against each other with their open hands and mirroring each other’s movements.

VOJ: Better than talking about feelings!

C: Yeah. Alright, we’ve already quoted the author a lot so let’s stop there.

VOJ: What? No closing quote?!

C: Yeah. Our podcast is starting to sound too much like fortune cookies.

VOJ: Or like those motivational posters.

C: Yeah.

VOJ: Or like a magic eight ball.

C: Okay, we get it.

VOJ: Or like birthday cards.

C: We get it!

VOJ: Or coffee mugs.

C: Okay stop!
