#74. No Drama Discipline Part 5.

Chris: Chapter 5 is about the 1-2-3 approach - one definition, two principles, and three desired outcomes.

Voice Of Judgement: It’s like we’re doing Sesame Street now.

C: It’s a simple way to remember everything the book has covered so far. The one definition is to remember that discipline is about teaching.

VOJ: Not punching. Got it.

C: The two principles are:

  1. Wait until your child is ready.
  2. Be consistent, but not rigid.
The first principle is about finding the sweet spot of engagement. You don’t want the person to be underaroused and bored, and you don’t want them overaroused and hyperactive.

VOJ: So make them zen.

C: Right. And also be zen yourself. The second principle is about knowing the difference between consistency and rigidity. Start by knowing what is non-negotiable, like physical safety. When you know that, then you know where you can make exceptions to other boundaries and allow more factors to come in to play.

VOJ: Okay, keep the kid alive. And then be nice.

C: Close enough. And the three mindsight outcomes are:

  1. Insight
  2. Empathy
  3. Integration and the repair of ruptures

VOJ: Man, these guys like lists.

C: Those outcomes are pretty self explanatory so we won’t go over them again.

VOJ: You know, I was reading another list. It was called “100 Things To Do Before You Die”. Surprisingly, “yell for help” wasn’t one of them.

C: Oh man, that’s terrible.

VOJ: Here’s another. A bear walks into a bar and says, “Give me a whiskey and … coke.” “Why the big pause?” asks the bartender. The bear shrugged. “I’m not sure; I was born with them.”

C: I feel like we’re going to get canceled.
