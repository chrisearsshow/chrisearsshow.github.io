#107. Connect Part 10.

Chris: Chapter 11 goes into detail about how to break a logjam in a relationship.

OV: This chapter really sucks.

C: Probably because it makes us so uncomfortable. Conflict isn't fun. But it helps to remember that others can influence us, but they need not control us.

OV: I'm still not convinced.

C: Okay, let's take the first baby step, which is to understand your own needs and feelings, and then speak those needs and feelings out loud. No one is a mind reader. Then and only then can you move into mutual problem-solving and repair.

OV: It's always about feelings!

C: Yup. After that comes the balance of responsibility.

OV: But it's not my fault!

C: Maybe. There can be a danger in not taking responsibility for the other person’s reactions. It means I'm not owning the fact that I'm in a relationship with the other person. Conversely, there can be danger in taking too much responsibility. That means the other person is a helpless victim. Again, the balance is holding both statements, "I have a choice" and "I am affected by you", as equally valid.

OV: So not one or the other.

C: Right. Even though conflict can feel stressful and even dangerous, it can actually be helpful. Conflict can surface issues in a very direct way. It can bring out emotions, indicating what’s really going on so you know where others stand.

OV: We should get into fights more often.

C: The next chapter goes into more detail about using conflict productively.

OV: I once got into a fight with 1,3,5,7 and 9. The odds were against me.

C: Sigh.

OV: They say you should never fight a math teacher. You’ll always be outnumbered.

C: Math fighting jokes? Really?

OV: They also say don't pick a fight with a dinosaur. Otherwise, you'll get jurasskicked.

C: This is why nobody listens to this podcast.
