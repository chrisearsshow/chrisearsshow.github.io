#90. Brainspotting Part 1.

Chris: In this episode, we’re zipping through a book by David Grand called “Brainspotting”.

Voice Of Judgement: Trainspotting?

C: No, brainspotting.

VOJ: Oh, so not heroin addicts.

C: Yeah, no. It’s a therapy that evolved out of EMDR.

VOJ: Yay, more eye movement.

C: Right, while EMDR is more about the technique, brainspotting incorporates more attunement.

VOJ: Like piano tuning?

C: Pretty close. A brainspotting therapist pays attention to where the client is looking and how different spots bring up different things for the client. One spot may be activating while another may be resourcing.

VOJ: That’s some pretty fancy words there.

C: Activating is related to disturbing thoughts and memories, and resourcing is related to calming.

VOJ: Then why not use those words?

C: I didn’t invent the therapy!

VOJ: Sounds pretty much like EMDR but with new words.

C: Well, brainspotting incorporates other variations like having only one eye open during the process or looking along the Y and Z axis.

VOJ: Y and Z axis sounds like a science experiment.

C: Up and down is the Y-axis. Near and far is the Z-axis.

VOJ: What do you call a dinosaur with no eyes?

C: Sigh.

VOJ: A ‘Do-you-think-he-saurus.’

C: Why? Why do we do this to ourselves?

VOJ: Come on! We love eye puns. The cornea the better!

C: Oh my god.
